I Did My Best, But My Dad Could Never Really Love Me!

It can be hard when your entire life has been a comparison to your sibling. What’s even worse is when the one comparing the two of you is none other but your dad.

Emily, 21, spent her entire childhood and teenage years in the shadow of big sister, Linda. She didn’t have much of a choice to be fair. Linda, was a beautiful 24-year-old who was trying to make it as an actor/model.

Emily, on the other hand, was just an everyday woman trying to make it as a writer.

“I remember playing with my sister all the time when we were kids. She was a few years older, so I always looked up to her. I remember the day I realized that my dad didn’t appreciate me as much… ”

We were playing in the backyard when my dad said “Hey princess, come sit here with me!”. So I ran to him but he immediately said “Not you! I meant your sister!”. To which I replied “You have to think of a nickname for me too dad”. ‘I don’t have time for this Emily!’, he said. That was when I knew!”, Emily explains.

I spent most of my teenage years in my room. I was always good at school, but of course, I wasn’t my sister! She was always the popular one, and for my dad that was more important. This made me resort to food even more than before.

Emily moved out of her parents’ house as soon as she graduated from college.

On Emily’s 21st birthday party>

I decided to celebrate my 21st birthday with my family. My sister was in town too, because her casting agency dropped her, so I thought we could all get together.

When I got there, my mom asked about my new job but before I even got a word out my dad said “No! Let’s hear about Linda’s stories first!”.

My sister of course started rumbling about her amazing life, so I just pretended I didn’t mind.

When my mom brought the cake, I blew out the candles and got a piece for me when my dad said “Sure you wanna do that? You’re not gonna look any better with that!”.

That was more than I could take! I started crying and then stormed out.

I decided it was time for me to make some changes. I’d had enough of my dad’s attitude towards me. I started writing down everything that I went through during my childhood, and even now, and before I knew it, I had written a couple of pages.

The next morning I took a look at what I’d written and thought that I could write a book about it!. And then it just hit me! I should write a book about this!. It took me about 3 months, but I even found a publisher that was willing to give it a go!

I never thought that it would become a best-seller!

I hadn’t seen my parents since my birthday, so I figured this would be a good time to do so. I went in and told them about the book I had written, without mentioning the plot. Then I went over to my dad and handed him a copy all wrapped up!

“Here dad! This is for you, I hope this time you’ll like what I did!”

My dad unwrapped it and read the title “Hi! My name is Daughter Number Two!”. I turned around and left with a smile on my face… that was the last time I saw him and, honestly, I’m happier without him!
